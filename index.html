Who We're For

Caring looks different for everyone. However you came to it, there's a place for you here.

Unpaid carers

Looking after a family member, partner or friend who needs support.

Paid carers

Care workers, personal assistants and home care staff who do this vital work as a job.

Parent carers & SEND families

Raising a child with special educational needs and additional disabilities.

Referrers

Social workers, GPs and care agencies who want to point someone in the right direction.

All are welcome — whatever your background, faith, or journey.

Our Story

Carers Community Circle started with a simple idea: caring can be isolating, and no one should have to carry it alone. We bring people together — over coffee, on a walk, in a workshop, or online — to share the load, swap knowledge, and remind each other that support is close by.

We support people from all cultural, ethnic, religious and socio-economic backgrounds, including unpaid and paid carers, vulnerable adults, older people, people experiencing mental health difficulties, loneliness or isolation, and families across Hackney and the surrounding boroughs — in person and online, wherever you're based.

Who we are, officially

Carers Community Circle is a Community Interest Company (CIC) — a social enterprise set up to put people before profit. That means everything we do is directed back into supporting our community, and our accounts are publicly available on the CIC register. We're not a registered charity, but we hold ourselves to the same standard of transparency and care.

What We Offer

Something for every stage of your caring journey — in person and online.

☕

Community & connection

Coffee mornings, peer support groups and discussion groups where you can share what you're going through, build real friendships, and feel a little less alone.

🏃

Wellbeing & activity

Walks, exercise sessions and wellbeing activities that look after your body as well as your mind — a reason to get outside, move, and reconnect.

🧠

The Knowledge Exchange

Our peer-led group where carers share what they've learned — from navigating services to everyday coping strategies — because sometimes the best advice comes from someone who's been there.

🎬

Dementia-friendly screenings

Relaxed, judgement-free film screenings designed with dementia in mind, so you and the person you care for can enjoy a morning out together.

🎓

Workshops & learning

Workshops, educational programmes, CPD, vocational and digital skills training — building confidence and opening doors, whether you're preparing for work, further study, or simply want to feel more confident online.

🚌

Trips & outings

Museum visits, theatre trips, swimming and community outings — because caring responsibilities shouldn't mean missing out on culture, fun and shared memories.

💻

Online & from home

Virtual coffee mornings, reading clubs, online peer support, mindfulness sessions and one-to-one mentoring — for anyone who can't always get to us in person, wherever you are.
